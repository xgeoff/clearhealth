Concierge & DPC Operating Model with ClearHealth

Combining a Direct Primary Care (DPC) or Concierge Medicine practice with your ClearHealth High Deductible Health Plan (HDHP) can create a powerful, patient-centered healthcare experience. It aligns incentives for preventive care while providing comprehensive coverage for major medical events.

Step by step legally and operationally structure: 1️⃣ legal/regulatory model, 2️⃣ contract relationships, 3️⃣ money flow, 4️⃣ how to position it in marketing and compliance materials.

⚖️ 1. Legal & Regulatory Model

You’ll be combining two entities under one “member experience,” but they must remain legally separate:

EntityLegal CategoryRole
ClearHealth Basic HDHPHigh Deductible Health Plan (insurance / self-funded plan)Covers major medical, hospitalization, specialty care, prescriptions (Cost Plus).
Concierge / DPC PracticeMedical service provider (non-insurance)Provides primary care access for a flat monthly/annual fee.

➡ The key to preserving HSA eligibility is that ClearHealth never directly pays for or bundles the concierge subscription as part of the insurance policy. The member pays that separately (or via payroll deduction if an employer offers both).

The IRS considers a plan “non-HSA eligible” if it provides first-dollar medical coverage before the deductible is met — so concierge fees must be kept outside the HDHP.

🧾 2. Contractual Relationships

You’ll need three simple, separate agreements:

A. Member ↔ ClearHealth (Insurance Agreement)

  • Standard HDHP terms (IRS-compliant, ERISA or state-regulated).
  • Member agrees to pay monthly premium.
  • Plan covers catastrophic and post-deductible medical expenses.
  • Defines reference pricing (120% of Medicare), Cost Plus drug pricing, deductible, etc.

B. Member ↔ Concierge Practice (Service Agreement)

  • A direct primary care contract (between patient and physician).
  • Describes the scope of included services (routine visits, messaging, labs).
  • Explicitly states it is not insurance and does not cover hospitalization, specialists, or emergencies.
  • Payment handled directly (credit card, ACH, or payroll deduction).
  • Optional clause: “This membership does not impact HSA eligibility for the ClearHealth HDHP.”

C. ClearHealth ↔ Concierge Practice (Marketing & Data-Sharing Agreement)

  • Defines a non-financial partnership:

    • Shared marketing (the practice can promote ClearHealth as a recommended catastrophic plan).
    • Optional data exchange (care coordination, referrals).
    • No shared revenue or bundled pricing (to preserve HSA status).

  • Optional referral incentive (fixed marketing fee, not percentage-based premium sharing).

💰 3. Money Flow

Here’s the simple, compliant flow of funds:

        Member
          │
   ┌──────┴────────┐
   │               │
ClearHealth      Concierge Practice
  (Premium)        (Subscription Fee)
   │               │
  Claim Fund → pays post-deductible care
                  │
        Provides primary care, labs, referrals

If the concierge clinic performs labs or procedures not included in their membership (e.g., imaging, biopsy), the member can:

  • Pay cash (counts toward deductible), or
  • Submit the invoice to ClearHealth once deductible is met.

🧩 4. Example Contract Language (Simplified)

Concierge Service Agreement (Excerpt)

This Agreement is between Dr. Jane Smith, MD (“Provider”) and John Doe (“Patient”). Provider agrees to make available routine and preventive medical services described herein for an annual fee of $1,200. This Agreement is not health insurance, does not cover hospital services, emergency care, or care provided by other physicians or facilities, and does not replace insurance coverage.

Patient acknowledges that their ClearHealth HDHP remains their primary insurance plan and that this Agreement does not affect their eligibility to contribute to a Health Savings Account (HSA).

ClearHealth – Provider Affiliation Agreement (Excerpt)

The Provider agrees to make ClearHealth members aware of the ClearHealth HDHP as an optional catastrophic coverage plan. Provider may disclose non-identifiable utilization data to ClearHealth for the purposes of coordination, analysis, and quality reporting. Provider shall not receive any portion of insurance premiums or act as an agent of the Plan.

🧠 5. Marketing and Positioning

For Members

“Keep your personal doctor. Get real insurance for the big stuff.” ClearHealth covers hospitals, specialists, and emergencies. Your DPC or concierge doctor covers everything else. Together, you get full-spectrum care with total price transparency.

For Doctors

“You’re already doing direct primary care. We give your patients affordable catastrophic protection — without making you deal with insurance.”

  • No network contracts.
  • No billing headaches.
  • Your patients stay loyal because they finally have coverage for major medical events.

For Investors

“ClearHealth bridges the gap between direct primary care and traditional insurance.”

  • Creates a hybrid model that aligns incentives across the system.
  • Reduces claim volume by 60–80%.
  • Partners with existing DPC networks for rapid distribution.
  • Preserves HSA compatibility (IRS HDHP compliant).

📊 6. Growth and Partnerships

You could start with pilot partnerships:

  • 3–5 concierge clinics or DPC practices in Washington and Oregon.
  • Enroll their panels into ClearHealth as a preferred catastrophic coverage.
  • Offer them co-branded marketing material: “ClearHealth + Dr. Smith Concierge Care.”
  • Use this as your distribution channel — physicians become your sales force.

By Year 2, integrate with existing DPC networks like:

  • Hint Health
  • Nextera Healthcare
  • Atlas MD
  • Elation Health (EHR with DPC support)

These platforms already handle billing, scheduling, and contracts — meaning integration is low-cost.
